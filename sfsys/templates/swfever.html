Death Rate from African Swine Flu

Domestic pigs get significant symptoms quickly,
and up to 100% of them die; there are strains
that have a lower fatality rate, but it is
still between 30% and 70%. Pigs who make it
through the acute phase appear to have a
good chance of recovering, but they might
be viral carriers for months.

Reference: Wageningen University and Research

Impacts of African Swine Flu on Pigs

According to the PSA, the Philippines' pig population has been reduced by about 3 million as a result of Asiatic Sprague and Swine Fever (ASF). Over 1,000 pigs in Solsona and Dingras town in Ilocos Norte were slaughtered to stop the spread of ASF. Approximately 3,000 pigs were killed in Cavite province on October 24, 2019 as part of the government's "depopulation"of hog farms in African swine fever-affected regions (ASF).
